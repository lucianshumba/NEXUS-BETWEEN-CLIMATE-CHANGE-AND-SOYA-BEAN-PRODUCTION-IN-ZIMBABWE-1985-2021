# Nexus Between Climate Change and Soya Bean Production in Zimbabwe (1985–2021)

## Overview
A statistical investigation into how climate change factors — rainfall and temperature — affect soya bean production in Zimbabwe, with production forecasts through 2030.

## Author
Lucia Nomatter Shumba — Master of Official Statistics (Specialized in Agriculture), Eastern Africa Statistical Training Centre (EASTC), 2023.

## Objectives
- Assess trends in climate parameters and soya bean production (1985–2021)
- Estimate the effect of rainfall and temperature on production
- Forecast soya bean production for 2023–2030

## Data
- **Sources:** FAOSTAT and World Bank
- **Period:** 1985–2021 (37 observations)
- **Variables:** Soya bean production (tons), harvested area (ha), annual mean temperature (°C), annual mean rainfall (mm)

## Methods
- Descriptive statistics & trend analysis
- Mann-Kendall test for trend detection
- Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) unit root tests
- ARDL bounds test for cointegration
- ARIMA (1,1,1) for forecasting

## Key Findings
- Soya bean production is on a **significant declining trend**
- Annual rainfall is **increasing** over the study period
- Temperature shows **no significant trend or impact** on production
- Rainfall has a **significant negative effect** on production in both short and long run
- Harvested area has a **significant positive effect** on production
- Production is forecast to **decline ~24%** by 2030 (from ~52,747 to ~40,085 metric tons)

## Tools
- Stata 16.1

## Relevance
Findings support policy decisions around food security, sustainable land use, and climate adaptation strategies in Zimbabwe's agricultural sector.
